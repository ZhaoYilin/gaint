1. Introduction

To begin a quantum chemistry calculation, first build the Hamiltonian of a system. In terms of second quantization operators, a time-independent non-relativistic Hamiltonian gives: \begin{equation} H = - \sum_{ij} t_{ij}\hat{c}^{\dagger}_{i}\hat{c}_{j} + \frac{1}{2} \sum_{ijkl} V_{ijkl}\hat{c}^{\dagger}_{i}\hat{c}^{\dagger}_{k}\hat{c}_{l}\hat{c}_{j} \end{equation}

There are many integration schemes including McMurchie–Davidson, Obara–Saika and Rys schemes. Here Obara-Saika and McMurchie–Davidson scheme is shown in this note.
